What is Mono?

Mono is a desktop application for Windows and macOS that records audio from any application — Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Discord, WhatsApp, and others — without joining calls as a bot. It performs AI transcription, speaker identification, and semantic search entirely on-device. Mono costs $50 as a one-time payment with no monthly subscription.

Key Features

  • Invisible recording — no bot joins your meeting

FAQ

Does Mono join my meeting as a bot?
No. Mono records audio directly from your computer's audio output, so it is completely invisible to other meeting participants. No bot joins your call.
Is my audio processed in the cloud?
No. All transcription, speaker identification, and search happens locally on your device using on-device AI. Your audio never leaves your computer.
Which apps does Mono work with?
Mono works with any application that plays audio on your computer, including Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Discord, WhatsApp, Telegram, and more.
Which platforms does Mono support?
Mono is available for Windows 10 and 11, and for macOS on both Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3) and Intel Macs.
How much does Mono cost?
Mono costs $50 as a one-time payment. There is no monthly subscription. The purchase includes lifetime updates.
Do I get updates after purchase?
Yes. Your one-time $50 purchase includes all future updates. The app updates automatically in the background.
How is Mono different from Otter or Fireflies?
Unlike Otter.ai and Fireflies.ai, Mono does not join your meeting as a bot, does not upload your audio to the cloud, and charges a one-time fee instead of a monthly subscription. All AI processing happens on your device.
