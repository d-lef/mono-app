How to Record Zoom Calls Without a Bot

Feb 23, 2026 · 8 min read · By Daniil Lefanov

Disclosure: Mono is our product. We've included it in this guide and tried to be fair, but you should know we have a stake in it.

Many Zoom recording tools work by joining your meeting as a bot participant — visible to everyone, often requiring host permission, and sending your audio to third-party servers. But there are ways to record Zoom calls locally, without any bot joining and without uploading your audio to the cloud.

Here are four methods for recording Zoom meetings in 2026, including options that keep your recordings completely private.

TL;DR

  • Best overall: Mono — no bot, local AI transcription, $50 once
  • Best free: OBS Studio — no bot, requires setup, no transcription
  • Built-in (visible): Zoom's native recording — notifies all participants
  • Bot-based: Otter.ai, Fireflies, Fathom — visible bot joins meeting

Method 1: Mono (Recommended)

Mono records Zoom by capturing audio directly from your computer's sound output. No bot joins the meeting, no recording notification appears to other participants, and your audio never leaves your computer. After the meeting, Mono transcribes the recording locally using AI.

How to record Zoom with Mono

  1. Download Mono from mono-ai.uk and install it
  2. Open Mono and click the Record button
  3. Join your Zoom meeting
  4. When the meeting ends, click Stop in Mono
  5. View your transcript with speaker labels and timestamps

Pros: No bot visible, no participant notification, local AI transcription, works even if you're not the host, one-time $50 payment.

Cons: Paid software (one free recording to try).

Method 2: OBS Studio (Free)

OBS Studio can capture system audio including Zoom calls. It doesn't join the meeting as a participant, so it's invisible to others. The main limitation is that it outputs raw audio/video files without transcription.

How to record Zoom with OBS

  1. Download OBS Studio from obsproject.com
  2. Go to Settings → Audio and set "Desktop Audio" to your playback device
  3. Add an Audio Output Capture source (or Display Capture for video)
  4. Click "Start Recording" before joining your Zoom meeting
  5. Click "Stop Recording" when the meeting ends

Pros: Completely free, no bot visible, can record video too.

Cons: Requires setup, must remember to start/stop manually, no transcription.

Method 3: Zoom's Built-in Recording

Zoom has native recording, but with limitations. Local recording requires host permission or being the host yourself. Cloud recording requires a paid Zoom plan. Most importantly, all participants see a "Recording" notification — there's no way to record discreetly with Zoom's built-in feature.

Pros: No additional software needed, cloud recording available on paid plans.

Cons: Visible notification to all participants, requires host permission, cloud recording requires paid Zoom subscription.

Method 4: Bot-Based Recorders (Otter.ai, Fireflies, Fathom)

Tools like Otter.ai, Fireflies, and Fathom work by joining your Zoom meeting as a bot participant. This means everyone in the meeting sees the bot join, and your audio is uploaded to their servers for transcription.

Pros: Easy setup, good transcription quality, team features available.

Cons: Bot is visible to all participants, audio uploaded to third-party cloud, monthly subscription fees ($10–30/month).

Tips for Recording Zoom Meetings

Use headphones to prevent echo. Close other apps that might make sounds. Test your setup before important meetings with a quick recording.

Which Method Should You Use?

Need transcription without a bot? Mono records locally and transcribes without anyone knowing.

Want free and don't need transcription? OBS Studio works but requires more setup.

Don't mind the notification? Zoom's built-in recording is the simplest option.

Team with existing subscription? Bot-based tools like Fathom or Otter.ai integrate with CRMs and offer team features.
