Otter.ai Alternatives in 2026: Which Meeting Recorder Actually Works?

Feb 21, 2026 · 8 min read

If you're shopping for a meeting recorder in 2026, the market is crowded. Otter.ai is the name everyone knows — but it's not right for everyone. Bots that join your calls visibly, audio uploaded to the cloud, and monthly fees that add up fast are all reasons people look elsewhere. This guide covers the five best alternatives, with honest takes on price, privacy, and what each tool actually does well.

Fireflies.ai

One of the most feature-rich options in this space. Fireflies joins your Zoom or Google Meet as a visible bot, records both sides, and gives you a transcript plus AI summaries. The free tier is very limited — you get 800 minutes of storage and AI features require a paid plan. At $10–19 per user per month, costs stack up for teams.

Best for: sales teams who want CRM integration and team-level access.

Fathom

Fathom is arguably the best free meeting recorder available right now. The free tier is genuinely useful — unlimited recordings, automatic summaries, and solid Zoom support. The paid plan ($29/mo) adds CRM sync and team features. Like Fireflies, it joins as a bot, so everyone in the call can see it.

Best for: individuals on a budget who primarily use Zoom and don't mind the bot.

Krisp

Krisp is primarily a noise-cancellation tool that added meeting recording as a secondary feature. It works as a virtual audio device rather than a bot, so it doesn't join calls visibly — but transcription and notes are processed in the cloud. At $16/month it's mid-priced.

Best for: people who need noise suppression and want basic transcription as a bonus.

Tactiq

Tactiq is a Chrome extension that captures captions from Google Meet, Zoom Web, and Teams Web — it doesn't record audio at all. It reads the auto-generated captions on-screen and compiles them into a transcript. This means it's limited to browser-based meetings and depends entirely on the quality of the platform's auto-captions. At $12/month it's affordable.

Best for: people who only do browser-based meetings and want a lightweight, no-install option.

Mono

Mono takes a different approach. Instead of joining calls as a bot or depending on browser captions, it records audio directly from your computer's audio output — completely invisibly to other participants. Transcription, speaker identification, and search all happen locally on your device using Whisper. Your audio never leaves your computer.

The unusual pricing: $50 once, no subscription, works with every app that plays audio (Zoom, Teams, Meet, Discord, WhatsApp, Telegram, anything).

Best for: people who care about privacy, record from multiple apps, or are tired of subscriptions.
