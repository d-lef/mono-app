WhatsApp Desktop doesn't have a built-in call recording feature. Whether you need to save an important business conversation, keep a record of a family call, or document a discussion, you'll need a third-party tool to record WhatsApp calls on your computer.

Method 1: Mono (Recommended)

Mono is a desktop application that records any audio playing on your computer, including WhatsApp voice and video calls. It captures audio directly from your system's sound output — no notification appears to the other person. After recording, Mono transcribes the conversation using local AI and identifies different speakers.

How to record WhatsApp calls with Mono

  1. Download Mono from mono-ai.uk and install it
  2. Open Mono and click the Record button
  3. Start your WhatsApp call
  4. When the call ends, click Stop in Mono
  5. View your transcript with speaker labels and timestamps

Pros: Works with voice and video calls, invisible to other participants, automatic transcription with speaker identification, searchable recordings, one-time $50 payment.

Cons: Paid software (one free recording to try).

Method 2: OBS Studio (Free)

OBS Studio is free, open-source software that can capture system audio. While it's primarily designed for streaming, it works well for recording WhatsApp calls too — just requires some initial configuration.

How to record WhatsApp calls with OBS

  1. Download OBS Studio from obsproject.com
  2. Open Settings → Audio
  3. Set "Desktop Audio" to your playback device
  4. Add an Audio Output Capture source
  5. Click "Start Recording" before your WhatsApp call
  6. Click "Stop Recording" when the call ends

Pros: Completely free, open source, can record video too.

Cons: Requires setup, must remember to start/stop manually, no transcription — just raw audio/video files.

Why WhatsApp Doesn't Have Built-in Recording

WhatsApp emphasizes privacy with features like end-to-end encryption. Adding built-in call recording would create privacy concerns, as recordings could be made without the other party's consent. Like Telegram and other privacy-focused apps, WhatsApp leaves recording to third-party tools.

Recording WhatsApp Video Calls

Both Mono and OBS can record WhatsApp video calls. Mono captures the audio portion with transcription; OBS can capture both audio and video if you add a Display Capture source. For most use cases where you just need the conversation content, audio-only recording with transcription (Mono) is more practical than full video.

Tips for Better WhatsApp Call Recordings

Use headphones to prevent echo. Ensure WhatsApp Desktop is connected to your phone before starting. Test with a short call before important conversations.

Which Method Should You Use?

If you want free: OBS Studio works but requires more effort and doesn't include transcription.

For business use: Mono's organized library and search features make it easier to find specific conversations later.
