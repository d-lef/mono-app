How to Record Discord Calls in 2026

Feb 23, 2026 · 8 min read · By Daniil Lefanov

Disclosure: Mono is our product. We've included it in this guide and tried to be fair, but you should know we have a stake in it.

Discord doesn't have a built-in recording feature for voice calls or voice chats. Whether you're recording a podcast, saving a gaming session, or keeping notes from a team discussion, you need a third-party tool to capture Discord calls. Here are four methods that actually work in 2026.

TL;DR

  • Best overall: Mono — invisible recording, local AI transcription, $50 once
  • Best free: OBS Studio — requires setup, no transcription
  • Best for podcasts: Craig Bot — per-speaker tracks, visible bot
  • Built-in: None — Discord has no native recording

Method 1: Mono (Recommended)

Mono records Discord calls by capturing audio directly from your computer's sound output. No bot joins the voice channel, no one sees a recording indicator, and the audio never leaves your computer. After recording, Mono transcribes the conversation locally using AI and lets you search through your recordings.

How to record Discord with Mono

  1. Download Mono from mono-ai.uk and install it
  2. Open Mono and click the Record button
  3. Join your Discord voice channel or call
  4. When finished, click Stop in Mono
  5. Mono transcribes the recording automatically

Pros: Invisible to other participants, works with DMs and servers, includes AI transcription and speaker identification, one-time $50 payment.

Cons: Paid software (free trial available with one recording limit).

Method 2: OBS Studio (Free)

OBS Studio is a free, open-source screen recorder that can capture system audio, including Discord calls. It requires some initial setup but works reliably once configured.

How to record Discord with OBS

  1. Download OBS Studio from obsproject.com
  2. Go to Settings → Audio
  3. Set "Desktop Audio" to your playback device
  4. Add an Audio Output Capture source
  5. Click "Start Recording" before your Discord call
  6. Click "Stop Recording" when done

Pros: Completely free, no bot visible, can also record video if needed.

Cons: Requires manual setup, must remember to start/stop, no transcription, outputs raw audio file only.

Method 3: Craig Bot (Server Channels Only)

Craig Bot is a Discord bot specifically designed for recording voice channels. It joins your server's voice channel as a visible bot and records each participant on a separate audio track — ideal for podcasts or interviews where you need to edit speakers individually.

How to record Discord with Craig Bot

  1. Invite Craig Bot to your server from craig.chat
  2. Join a voice channel
  3. Type /join in a text channel
  4. Craig joins and starts recording
  5. Type /stop when finished
  6. Download the recording from the link Craig provides

Pros: Free tier available, multi-track recording (separate file per speaker), good for podcasts.

Cons: Only works in server voice channels (not DMs), bot is visible to everyone, no transcription, requires bot permissions on server.

Tips for Better Discord Recordings

Use headphones to prevent echo. Close other audio apps to avoid capturing unwanted sounds. Test your setup with a short recording before important calls.

Which Method Should You Use?

For private calls or DMs: Use Mono or OBS — Craig Bot only works in server voice channels.

For podcast recording: Craig Bot's multi-track output is ideal for editing. Alternatively, Mono with its speaker identification works well too.

For regular meeting notes: Mono's automatic transcription saves time compared to listening back through raw audio.

For free recording: OBS Studio works but requires manual effort and doesn't include transcription.
