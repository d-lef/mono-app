How to Record Google Meet Without a Bot

Feb 23, 2026 · 8 min read · By Daniil Lefanov

Disclosure: Mono is our product. We've included it in this guide and tried to be fair, but you should know we have a stake in it.

Google Meet's built-in recording is only available on paid Google Workspace accounts — and even then, it notifies all participants. Many third-party tools work by joining your meeting as a bot, which everyone can see. But there are ways to record Google Meet locally, without bots and without uploading audio to the cloud.

Here are four methods for recording Google Meet in 2026, including options for free Google accounts.

TL;DR

  • Best overall: Mono — no bot, local AI transcription, $50 once
  • Best free: OBS Studio — no bot, requires setup, no transcription
  • Built-in: Google Meet recording — requires Workspace paid plan, notifies participants
  • Browser extension: Tactiq — captures captions only, no audio recording

Method 1: Mono (Recommended)

Mono records Google Meet by capturing audio directly from your computer's sound output. No bot joins the meeting, no notification appears, and your audio stays on your computer. After the meeting, Mono transcribes the recording locally using AI and identifies speakers.

How to record Google Meet with Mono

  1. Download Mono from mono-ai.uk and install it
  2. Open Mono and click the Record button
  3. Join your Google Meet meeting
  4. When the meeting ends, click Stop in Mono
  5. View your transcript with speaker labels and timestamps

Pros: Works with free Google accounts, no bot visible, no participant notification, local AI transcription, one-time $50 payment.

Cons: Paid software (one free recording to try).

Method 2: OBS Studio (Free)

OBS Studio can capture system audio including Google Meet calls. It's free, doesn't join the meeting as a participant, and works with any Google account.

How to record Google Meet with OBS

  1. Download OBS Studio from obsproject.com
  2. Go to Settings → Audio and set "Desktop Audio" to your playback device
  3. Add an Audio Output Capture source (or Display Capture for video)
  4. Click "Start Recording" before joining Google Meet
  5. Click "Stop Recording" when the meeting ends

Pros: Completely free, no bot, works with any Google account.

Cons: Requires setup, must start/stop manually, no transcription.

Method 3: Google Meet's Built-in Recording

Google Meet has native recording, but only for Google Workspace accounts on Business Standard tier or higher. Free Gmail accounts cannot use this feature. When recording, all participants see a notification that the meeting is being recorded.

Pros: No additional software, recording saved to Google Drive, includes transcript.

Cons: Requires paid Workspace account, all participants are notified, only organizers can start recording.

Method 4: Tactiq (Browser Extension)

Tactiq is a Chrome extension that captures Google Meet's auto-generated captions — it doesn't record audio. It compiles the live captions into a transcript you can export. Quality depends entirely on Meet's caption accuracy.

Pros: No audio recording (privacy advantage), works in browser, affordable at $12/month.

Cons: No actual audio recording, quality depends on Meet's captions, Chrome only.

Tips for Better Google Meet Recordings

Use headphones to improve audio clarity. Enable captions in Meet if using Tactiq. Close other browser tabs to avoid notification sounds in your recording.

Which Method Should You Use?

Free Google account + need transcription? Mono is the only option that works without Workspace.

Free Google account + don't need transcription? OBS Studio works but requires setup.

Have Workspace + don't mind notification? Google's built-in recording is simplest.

Only need text, not audio? Tactiq captures captions without recording.
