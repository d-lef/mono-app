How to Record Zoom Without a Bot Joining Your Meeting

Feb 22, 2026 · 4 min read · By Daniil Lefanov

Disclosure: Mono is our own product. We've included it in this guide and tried to be fair, but you should know we have a stake in it.

You're on a Zoom call and "Fireflies Notetaker" joins as a participant. Everyone notices. Someone asks who that is. You explain it's just an AI recorder. The vibe shifts.

Bot-based recorders work — but they're visible to everyone in the meeting, your audio goes to third-party servers, and the host can remove the bot mid-call. For client meetings, legal calls, or anything confidential, that's a problem. Here are three ways to record Zoom without one.

TL;DR

  • Free, hosts only, no transcript: Zoom native recording
  • Free, fully local, no transcript: OBS Studio — ~5 min setup

Zoom's built-in recording

Zoom has local recording built in. Click Record → Record on this Computer and a video file saves locally when the call ends. No bot, nothing sent to Zoom's cloud.

The catch: you need host permissions. Participants can only record if the host explicitly enables it for them. And the output is a video file — no transcript, no speaker labels, no way to search for something said 40 minutes in.

Best for: hosts who just need a video file of the call.

OBS Studio

OBS captures screen and system audio. Free, open source, nothing leaves your machine.

Setup: open OBS → Sources → add Display Capture for screen and Audio Output Capture for call audio. Hit Start Recording before the call, Stop when done. You get a local video file.

Same limitations as Zoom native: no transcript, no search. OBS also captures your entire screen — switching windows mid-call will show in the recording.

Best for: people who already use OBS, or want something fully free with no data leaving the machine.

Mono

After the call, it transcribes locally using Whisper: speaker labels, clickable timestamps, AI summary. All your recordings are searchable by meaning, not just keywords.

Works the same for Teams, Meet, Discord, WhatsApp — anything playing audio through your system. You don't configure anything per call.

Best for: anyone who needs a transcript, or records calls regularly and wants a searchable archive. Works regardless of whether you're the host.
