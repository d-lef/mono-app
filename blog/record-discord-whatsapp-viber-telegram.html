How to Record Discord, WhatsApp, Viber or Telegram Calls on Desktop

Feb 21, 2026 · 6 min read · By Daniil Lefanov

Disclosure: Mono is our own product. We've included it in this guide and tried to be fair, but you should know we have a stake in it.

If you're trying to record Discord, WhatsApp, Viber or Telegram calls on desktop, you've already hit the same wall: none of these apps have a Record button. Unlike Zoom or Teams — which at least offer native recording — messaging apps treat voice as ephemeral by design. That's a problem if you're capturing interviews, community sessions, client calls, or anything else you need to revisit. This guide covers what actually works, what's overkill, and what to reach for if you record regularly across more than one of these apps.

OBS Studio

OBS is a free, open-source recording and streaming tool originally built for gamers and content creators. It can capture system audio — anything playing through your speakers or headphones — which makes it technically capable of recording calls from any desktop app, including all four of these.

The catch: OBS is built for streamers and video producers, not for people who need a quick call recording. Getting it right means configuring audio sources, choosing output formats, and launching it manually before every session. There's no transcription, no speaker labels, no search. If you already use OBS for streaming or content work, it's a reasonable choice — you already know the interface. If you're installing it specifically to record a WhatsApp call, it's a lot of software for a simple job.

Best for: people who already run OBS for other purposes and want one tool to cover everything.

Craig Bot (Discord only)

Craig is a Discord bot built specifically for recording voice channels. You invite it to your server once, tell it to join a channel, and it records each participant on a separate audio track — genuinely useful for podcasts, community events, or any situation where you'll want to edit individual speakers later.

The limitations are real: Craig only works with Discord voice channels, not private calls. Everyone in the channel sees the bot in the participant list. Recordings are processed on Craig's servers and you download them from a web link. The free tier caps recording length and how long files are kept. And if your calls aren't on Discord, Craig doesn't help at all.

Best for: Discord communities, podcasters, and gaming groups who want per-speaker tracks and don't mind a visible bot.

Mono

Mono takes a different approach to all of the above. Instead of adding a bot to a channel or running a full production suite, it records audio directly from your computer's audio output — invisibly, without joining any call or channel as a participant. Because it captures at the system level, Discord, WhatsApp Desktop, Viber, and Telegram Desktop all work identically. No per-app configuration. No per-call setup. If it makes sound on your computer, Mono can record it.

After the call you get a local audio file, AI transcription, speaker identification, and semantic search — all processed on your device using Whisper. Your audio never leaves your computer, and there's no cloud account to manage.

The pricing is a one-time $50. No subscription, no monthly fee, no per-minute charge.

Best for: anyone who records across multiple apps, needs transcription, or wants nothing to configure before each call.
